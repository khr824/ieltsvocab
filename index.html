🔹 PART 1: Reading (Short Passage + Questions)

In recent years, food culture has changed significantly. In many cities, there is an abundant supply of international cuisine, allowing people to explore new flavours. While some prefer savory dishes with bold spices, others enjoy more mild meals that are easier on the palate.

However, not all food is of high quality. Poor storage can lead to spoilage, especially for perishable items like dairy and seafood. For example, milk can become soured, and bread may turn moldy if not consumed in time.

Despite these challenges, many people still enjoy cooking at home. Some like to savor every bite, while others quickly devour their meals after a long day. Cooking techniques such as stirring sauces properly or allowing chocolate to melt evenly can greatly improve the final result.

Overall, food remains an essential and enjoyable part of daily life, whether portions are generous or meager.

My eating habits rely heavily on a balance of grains, fresh produce, and seafood. I tend to prefer savory and zesty flavors over mild ones, often adding spices to my meals. Because I buy a lot of fresh, perishable ingredients like dairy and fish, I am very mindful of proper storage to prevent spoilage.
